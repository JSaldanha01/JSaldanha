## **Background of the Internship Program** {#background-of-the-internship-program}

As a Information System student with a concentration in Enterprise Resource Planning at President University, I am required to complete an internship as part of the academic curriculum. This program serves as an essential bridge between classroom learning and real-world workplace practices. Through the internship, students can gain a clearer understanding of how theories and concepts in international business are applied in actual working environments.  
The internship also provides opportunities to develop professional skills, including communication, teamwork, and analytical thinking, all of which are crucial for future career development. It allows students to experience how companies operate, how business decisions are made, and how to adapt within a professional setting. PT Shibly was selected as the internship placement due to its involvement in multiple business projects with clients in Timor-Leste, which offered valuable exposure to international business operations and cross-border collaboration.

## **Objective of the Internship** {#objective-of-the-internship}

The main objective of my internship is to gain practical experience that complements my academic studies. Through this program, I aim to:

1. Apply the theories and knowledge learned in university to real working environments.

      3. Improve analytical, problem-solving, and interpersonal skills through direct involvement in company projects.  
      4. Learn how to communicate and work effectively within a team in a professional setting.  
      5. Gain deeper insight into international business, especially related to the collaboration between Indonesia and Timor-Leste through PT Shibly's projects.

## **Benefits of the Internship** {#benefits-of-the-internship}

This internship has provided numerous professional and personal benefits. It strengthened the connection between academic learning and real business practices, offering practical experience in handling tasks, meeting deadlines, and communicating effectively in a professional environment. The experience also provides opportunities to explore potential career paths in international business.  
Throughout the internship, confidence was built in working independently and making decisions based on careful analysis. Overall, this internship has been a valuable learning experience that has prepared the individual well for entering the professional world.

## **History of the Company** {#history-of-the-company}

STS is Indonesia's leading IT system integration and services provider, supporting customers in planning, building, and managing their IT infrastructure with a combination of strong technical competencies and comprehensive solutions. Over the years, the company has established a presence in three countries and has continuously expanded its capabilities to meet evolving market demand.

STS began its journey with the company's launch and early involvement in major technology initiatives, including a significant project with Ericsson in 2016\. In 2017, the company successfully penetrated the Timor-Leste market, marking the start of its regional expansion. The momentum continued in 2018 with the Digital Customer Services Project, which strengthened its reputation in delivering innovative digital solutions. In 2019, STS expanded its portfolio by undertaking News, Entertainment, and Advertisement projects, demonstrating its ability to serve diverse industry needs.

Further growth was achieved in 2020, when the company entered the Australian market, demonstrating its international competitiveness. By 2021, STS set its sights on the Middle East market, seeking new opportunities and reinforcing its vision to become a global IT solutions provider.

# **CHAPTER III INTERNSHIP ACTIVITIES**

During the internship, I worked as a Frontend Programmer Intern at PT Shibly. My responsibilities primarily focused on frontend development, including UI design, UI implementation, bug fixing, system testing, and support for client-based projects. I was involved in several enterprise and telecommunication systems, such as Telkomcel’s Helpdesk, CRM360, Online Charging System (OCS), Advice Monitor, Zone Module, and BRI-related web applications, serving mainly Telkomcel and BRI clients.  
My tasks progressed from system orientation and UI evaluation to active frontend development, participation in client meetings, and involvement in User Acceptance Testing (UAT). Through these activities, I gained hands-on experience in applying frontend development principles within a professional IT and enterprise system environment.

## **Activities, Methods, and Approaches** {#activities,-methods,-and-approaches}

## **Activities** {#activities}

The main activities during the internship were centered on frontend development and user interface optimization. These activities included reviewing existing frontend interfaces, identifying UI defects and usability issues, and implementing interface improvements based on functional requirements and client feedback. I worked on multiple systems, including Helpdesk, CRM360, Online Charging System (OCS), Advice Monitor, Zone Module, and BRI-related web applications.

Additional activities involved implementing and modifying UI components, adjusting layouts and visual assets, and fixing frontend-related bugs. I also enhanced UI functionality by implementing input validation logic, data filtering mechanisms, dashboard visualizations (charts), and UI animations to improve user interaction and system usability.

Furthermore, I actively participated in internal development meetings and client coordination meetings with Telkomcel, supported User Acceptance Testing (UAT) activities, and resolved frontend issues identified during testing phases. Alongside project tasks, I continuously developed my technical competencies by learning and re-learning Flutter and JavaScript, as well as reviewing existing project source code to better understand application architecture, coding standards, and best practices.

## **Methods** {#methods}

The methods applied during the internship were based on frontend software development and system engineering practices. I began by reviewing existing source code and system documentation to understand application flow, component structure, and frontend architecture. Frontend development tools and frameworks were utilized to implement UI updates and feature enhancements.

Debugging and testing techniques were applied to identify and resolve UI-related issues, including inspecting browser console logs, tracing frontend logic, and testing user interaction scenarios. API documentation using Swagger was used to consume backend services and integrate dynamic data into frontend components, particularly for dashboard and monitoring modules.

Testing methods included manual UI testing, functional testing during development, and validation during UAT sessions with clients. Professional communication methods were consistently applied when reporting development progress, discussing technical issues with team members, and receiving feedback from clients to ensure alignment with project requirements.

## **Approaches** {#approaches}

A detail-oriented and systematic development approach was applied to ensure accuracy in UI implementation, frontend logic, and visual consistency across applications. Each interface change was carefully reviewed to minimize UI defects, prevent unintended behavior, and maintain alignment with existing system functionality.

A user-centered approach guided frontend development activities, emphasizing usability, clarity, and efficient user interaction. UI improvements, validation logic, and dashboard visualizations were designed to enhance user experience and reduce potential user errors.

A collaborative and communicative approach was maintained through regular coordination with team members and participation in internal and client meetings. Feedback from supervisors and clients, particularly during User Acceptance Testing (UAT) sessions, was actively incorporated into frontend improvements.

In addition, a proactive and adaptive learning approach was adopted when working with unfamiliar frameworks, new system modules, and evolving project requirements. Continuous self-learning, source code review, and experimentation supported effective problem-solving and enabled meaningful contributions to ongoing frontend development tasks.

# **Evaluation** {#evaluation-1}

Evaluation is necessary to identify strengths and weaknesses for future improvement. After completing an internship at PT Shibly, several key points can be highlighted:

## **Preparation**

At the beginning of the internship, I realized that my preparation in several technical areas was limited, particularly in using specific frontend frameworks and libraries such as React and Tailwind, which were completely new to me. I also needed time to fully understand the architecture of enterprise-level systems. In addition, adapting to professional technical terminology and workplace communication styles required extra effort, especially since Bahasa Indonesia is the primary language used in the office and I am not fully fluent.  
This experience emphasized the importance of strong technical and language preparation before entering a professional environment. Adequate preparation would allow interns to adapt more quickly and contribute effectively from the early stages of the internship.

## 

## **Active Participation**

I learned that active participation such as engaging in discussions, listening attentively, asking questions, and showing enthusiasm played a significant role in helping me adapt to the company’s work culture. Although I consider myself somewhat introverted, I made a conscious effort to communicate more openly, even though this remains an area for further improvement.  
By volunteering to assist with tasks and expressing interest in learning new skills, I was able to build trust with team members and receive guidance from more experienced colleagues. I also occasionally joined meetings outside my direct project scope, including discussions related to business and other technical fields, which broadened my understanding of how different teams collaborate within the organization.

## **Communication**

Since Bahasa Indonesia is the main language used in daily communication, I initially experienced difficulty expressing my ideas clearly, which sometimes slowed down my work. I also recognized the need to improve my ability to articulate technical terms and complex concepts, as precise communication is essential in the IT field to avoid misunderstandings.  
Through continuous practice, observation, and willingness to learn, my communication skills gradually improved. Over time, I became more confident when discussing technical matters with team members. This experience highlighted the importance of clear, structured, and confident communication in a professional IT environment.

## **Self-learning**

During the internship, I learned that not all tasks and concepts are explained in a step-by-step manner. As a result, I had to take the initiative to study unfamiliar technologies independently, particularly React and TypeScript, which required learning from the fundamentals.  
This self-learning process strengthened my independence, problem-solving ability, and adaptability. It enabled me to understand new technologies more efficiently and apply them to real project tasks, reinforcing the importance of continuous learning in the IT field

## **Adapting**

Tasks during the internship often changed in response to project needs, requiring me to adapt quickly. Whether it was analysing financial data, preparing a presentation, or reviewing business documents, I had to stay flexible and open to learning new things. This experience taught me the importance of adaptability in a real work environment

## **Recommendation for President University** {#references}

##  {#references}

While the ERP concentration naturally aligns with consulting-based work, the internship experience shows that project-based learning using real consulting simulations should be applied across all Computer Science concentrations, not only ERP. In industry, graduates are expected to analyze problems, design system solutions, and implement them in real organizational contexts, regardless of specialization.

It is therefore recommended that the students adopt project-based learning where students act as consultant for a fictitious company, tailored to their respective concentrations. Through this approach, students would analyze business or system problems, design appropriate solutions, and implement system components or prototypes. This method would better prepare students from all concentrations to meet industry expectations and perform more effectively during internships and early professional careers.

## **Recommendation for Institutions** {#references}

##  {#references}

For host institutions, especially those operating in IT and system integration fields, it is recommended to provide a structured technical onboarding program for interns at the beginning of the internship period. Introducing interns to system architecture, development tools, coding standards, and project workflows would enable them to adapt more quickly and work more efficiently.

Such onboarding would also help interns better understand their roles and expectations, allowing them to contribute more effectively to ongoing IT projects while reducing the initial learning curve. This approach would benefit both the interns and the organization by improving productivity and collaboration throughout the internship period.
